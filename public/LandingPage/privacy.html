Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 July 2026 • Effective: 9 July 2026

1. Introduction

Blaze Frontier ("we", "us", "our", or the "Platform") is an independent, community-driven esports tournament platform operated from India. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and what rights you have regarding your information. By accessing or using Blaze Frontier, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

2. Data Controller

Blaze Frontier is the data controller for personal information collected through this Platform. For any privacy-related enquiries, you may reach us at blazefrontierofficial@gmail.com.

3. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

3.1 Account Data (via Google OAuth)
When you sign in through Google, we receive your Google account ID, display name, email address, and profile picture. We do not receive or store your Google password.

3.2 In-Game Identity
During onboarding, you provide your in-game name (IGN) and Free Fire unique player ID (UID). These are used to verify your identity in tournament lobbies and to display publicly on leaderboards.

3.3 Location Data
We request geolocation access (via your browser's Geolocation API) during onboarding to verify your region. We store only the detected country/region name — not precise GPS coordinates. This is used solely for regional server assignment and eligibility checks.

3.4 User-Uploaded Content
You may upload gameplay video clips and profile pictures. These files are stored on our servers and may be displayed publicly on the Platform (e.g., highlight reels, profile pages).

3.5 Platform Activity Data
We collect data generated by your use of the Platform, including tournament participation records, BlazeCoins and BlazePoints balances, login streaks, matchmaking history, directive completions, and activity logs.

3.6 Session and Cookie Data
We use server-side session cookies (via express-session) for admin panel authentication and JWT (JSON Web Tokens) stored in localStorage for player authentication. These contain session identifiers and authentication tokens — not personal data beyond what is necessary for session management.

3.7 Automatically Collected Technical Data
Our server and hosting infrastructure may automatically log standard technical information such as your IP address, browser type, device type, referring URL, and timestamps. This data is used for security, rate limiting, and debugging purposes only.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on one or more of the following legal grounds:

• Consent: By creating an account and completing onboarding, you expressly consent to the collection and processing described in this Policy.
• Contractual Necessity: Processing is necessary to provide you with Platform services (tournament entry, leaderboard rankings, matchmaking).
• Legitimate Interest: We process certain data (e.g., activity logs, IP addresses) to maintain Platform security, prevent fraud, enforce fair play, and improve our services.

5. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected data for the following purposes:

• To create and manage your account
• To verify your identity and region for tournament eligibility
• To display player statistics, rankings, and leaderboards
• To facilitate matchmaking (including "Find a Squad" functionality)
• To manage virtual rewards (BlazeCoins, BlazePoints, badges)
• To display user-submitted content (clips, profile pictures)
• To enforce our Terms of Service, Fair Play Protocol, and anti-cheat policies
• To communicate with you regarding your account or Platform updates
• To detect and prevent abuse, fraud, and unauthorized access
• To improve and optimize Platform performance

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party.

We may share limited data only in these circumstances:

• Google OAuth: Authentication is handled by Google's Identity Services. Google's own privacy policy governs data processed by Google during sign-in.
• Public Display: Your in-game name, UID, profile picture, tournament results, rankings, BlazePoints, and uploaded clips are displayed publicly on the Platform. This is fundamental to the Platform's community purpose.
• Legal Obligations: We may disclose data if required by applicable law, court order, or governmental authority.
• Platform Protection: We may disclose data if necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Blaze Frontier, its users, or the public.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account remains active. If you request account deletion, we will delete or anonymise your personal data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law or necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims. Uploaded content (clips, profile pictures) will be permanently deleted upon account deletion.

8. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including:

• HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit
• Secure session management and JWT-based authentication
• Rate limiting on API endpoints to prevent abuse
• Access controls restricting administrative operations
• Regular security reviews of our codebase

However, no internet transmission is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security and encourage you to protect your own account credentials.

9. Children's Privacy

Blaze Frontier does not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal data to us, please contact us at blazefrontierofficial@gmail.com, and we will promptly delete such data.

10. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

• Right to Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
• Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
• Right to Deletion: Request deletion of your personal data and account.
• Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account or contacting us.
• Right to Data Portability: Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
• Right to Grievance Redressal: Under India's DPDP Act, you have the right to file a complaint regarding data processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at blazefrontierofficial@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

11. Third-Party Services

Our Platform integrates with or links to the following third-party services. Each is governed by its own privacy policy, which we encourage you to review:

• Google Identity Services (authentication) — Google Privacy Policy
• Google Fonts (typography) — Google Privacy Policy
• Discord (community links) — Discord Privacy Policy

We do not control and are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third-party services.

12. Compliance with Applicable Laws

Blaze Frontier is committed to complying with applicable data protection and privacy laws, including but not limited to:

• India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)
• Information Technology Act, 2000 and its associated rules
• General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable to users in the European Economic Area

13. Regional Availability and Geolocation Restriction

Blaze Frontier is currently available exclusively to users located in India. During onboarding, we verify your location via browser geolocation. Users detected outside India are restricted from completing account setup and accessing Platform services. This restriction is enforced both on the client side and server side. We reserve the right to expand or further restrict regional availability at any time without prior notice.

14. International Data Transfers

Our servers may be located outside your country of residence. By using the Platform, you consent to the transfer, storage, and processing of your data in India and/or other jurisdictions where our hosting infrastructure operates. We ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place for any such transfer.

15. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. Material changes will be communicated via a prominent notice on the Platform or via email where practicable. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the date of the most recent revision. Your continued use of the Platform after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

16. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or your personal data, please contact us at:
